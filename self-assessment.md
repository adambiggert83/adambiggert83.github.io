# Professional Self-Assessment


## Introduction

Completing the Bachelor of Science in Computer Science program and developing this ePortfolio has provided me with an opportunity to reflect on my growth as both a student and an information technology professional. Throughout the program, I have developed technical skills in software engineering, data structures and algorithms, databases, security, and system design while also strengthening professional skills such as communication, collaboration, problem solving, and project management.

This ePortfolio represents not only the knowledge I gained through coursework but also my ability to apply that knowledge to real-world challenges. As someone currently working as a Project Manager in a public-sector IT department and pursuing a career as an Information Security Analyst, I have found that the Computer Science program has helped me build a stronger technical foundation and better understand the technologies that support modern organizations.


## Collaboration and Communication

One of the most valuable aspects of the Computer Science program has been the emphasis on collaboration and communication. Successful technology projects require more than technical expertise; they require the ability to work effectively with diverse stakeholders and communicate complex information clearly.

Throughout the program, I participated in projects that involved:

* Requirements gathering
* System analysis
* Software design
* Technical presentations

These experiences reinforced the importance of translating technical concepts into language that users, managers, and decision makers can understand.

My professional experience as an IT Project Manager has further strengthened these skills by requiring me to communicate with:

* Technical teams
* Leadership
* Vendors
* End users

The ability to bridge the gap between technical and non-technical stakeholders is an essential skill that I will continue to utilize throughout my career.


## Software Engineering

The program also strengthened my understanding of software engineering principles and best practices. Through coursework and capstone enhancements, I learned the importance of:

* Modularity
* Maintainability
* Scalability
* Defensive programming

The **Software Design and Engineering Enhancement** in this portfolio demonstrates these principles by refactoring a course advising system into a more modular, object-oriented architecture.

By separating functionality into multiple classes, improving validation, and enhancing maintainability, I gained practical experience applying professional software development practices. This enhancement illustrates my ability to evaluate an existing solution and improve its quality through thoughtful design decisions.


## Data Structures and Algorithms

Another important area of growth involved data structures and algorithms. Understanding how information is organized, stored, and processed is fundamental to computer science.

Throughout the program, I gained experience working with:

* Vectors
* Linked Lists
* Hash Tables
* Trees
* Searching Algorithms
* Sorting Algorithms

For my **Algorithms and Data Structures Enhancement**, I expanded the course advising system's functionality by implementing graph-based prerequisite analysis.

Using:

* Graph relationships
* Recursive traversal
* Depth-First Search (DFS)

I developed a more sophisticated solution that can analyze course dependencies and detect circular prerequisite relationships.

This enhancement demonstrates my ability to evaluate algorithmic trade-offs and implement data structures that solve increasingly complex problems.


## Databases

Database design and management also became an important area of study throughout the program. Modern organizations rely heavily on data-driven systems, making effective data management an essential skill for technology professionals.

During my **Database Enhancement**, I integrated SQLite into the course advising system to replace the original file-based storage approach.

This enhancement introduced:

* Relational database concepts
* Persistent data storage
* Structured Query Language (SQL)
* Prepared statements

By designing normalized tables and implementing secure database interactions, I gained practical experience applying database technologies within a software application.

This experience reinforced the importance of:

* Data integrity
* Scalability
* Efficient information management

Courses on:

* Secure Coding

helped me understand how vulnerabilities can be introduced and mitigated through thoughtful design and implementation.

The enhancements included in this portfolio demonstrate security-focused practices such as:

* Input validation

These concepts are directly applicable to my goal of becoming an **Information Security Analyst**, where protecting systems, managing risk, and securing organizational data are primary responsibilities.
